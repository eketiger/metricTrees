---
title: Metric trees
description: How metric trees are structured in Metrics.
---

A metric tree is a directed graph of nodes whose edges represent causal or compositional
relationships between metrics. Inputs flow UP to outputs.

**Node types**

- `input` — a raw measured value.
- `formula` — derived via an expression referencing other nodes' slugs.
- `output` — a top-level goal or north star.
- `segment` — a breakdown of a metric.
- `strategy` — an initiative or bet.
- `annotation` — contextual note.
